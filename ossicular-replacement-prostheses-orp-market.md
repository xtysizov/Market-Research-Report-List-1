<p><p>Ossicular Replacement Prostheses (ORP) are medical devices used to restore hearing by replacing damaged ossicles in the middle ear during surgical procedures, typically due to conditions like cholesteatoma or otosclerosis. The market is divided into two main types: Partial Ossicular Replacement Prostheses (PORP), which replace one or more but not all ossicles, and Total Ossicular Replacement Prostheses (TORP), which replace the entire ossicular chain. Both types aim to improve sound conduction and enhance auditory function in patients.</p></p>
