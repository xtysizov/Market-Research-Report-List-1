<p><p>Long-Term Evolution (LTE) for the Internet of Things (IoT) includes two primary market types: Narrowband IoT (NB-IoT) and LTE-M (LTE Machine-Type Communications). NB-IoT is designed for low-power, low-data-rate devices, offering extensive coverage and battery life, making it ideal for smart meters and environmental sensors. In contrast, LTE-M supports higher data rates and mobility, catering to applications like connected vehicles and wearable devices. Both technologies enable efficient and scalable IoT connectivity by leveraging existing LTE infrastructure.</p></p>
