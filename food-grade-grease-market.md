<p><p>The food grade grease market consists of several lubricant categories, each serving specific applications. H1 lubricants are safe for incidental food contact and are used in areas where food is processed. H2 lubricants are suitable for general equipment lubrication but must not come into contact with food. 3H lubricants function as releasing agents, preventing food from sticking to surfaces. H3 lubricants, or soluble oils, are designed for cleaning or cooling purposes. Other market offerings may include specialty greases and compounds tailored for unique food industry requirements.</p></p>
