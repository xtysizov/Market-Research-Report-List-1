<p><p>The Oral Antidiabetic (OAD) treatment market comprises several key categories. Sulphonylureas stimulate insulin release from the pancreas, effectively lowering blood sugar levels. Biguanides, such as metformin, work by increasing insulin sensitivity and decreasing hepatic glucose production. Intestinal α-Glucosidase inhibitors slow carbohydrate absorption in the intestines, delaying glucose entry into the bloodstream. Other categories may include newer agents or combinations such as DPP-4 inhibitors and SGLT2 inhibitors, each targeting blood glucose control through different mechanisms.</p></p>
