<p><p>The New Energy Vehicle (NEV) motor thermal management market comprises two primary types: air cooling and liquid cooling. Air cooling utilizes ambient air to dissipate heat from the motor, offering a lightweight and simpler design, although it may be less effective in high-performance applications. Liquid cooling, on the other hand, circulates a coolant liquid around the motor, providing more efficient heat transfer and better temperature control, making it suitable for high-performance and electric vehicles that generate significant heat during operation.</p></p>
