<p><p>LiDAR for the automotive market is categorized into two main types: Mechanical LiDAR and Solid State LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR utilizes rotating components to capture a 360-degree view of the environment, offering high accuracy and long-range detection; however, it can be more cumbersome and prone to wear. Solid State LiDAR, on the other hand, employs fixed components and no moving parts, resulting in a more compact, durable design with lower costs, making it increasingly popular for integration into advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous vehicles.</p></p>
